Introduction

This is a very quick and dirty way to do reduce noise on an audio recording, and to trim moments of silence within the recording.
This effort was done in Python3 using LibROSA and pysndfx. These Python packages were chosen for the job because they use simple and efficient implementation of well known algorithms, and since (as far as I understand) we are allowed to use them, while giving them a mention/credit.
A few things to keep in mind about noise reduction:
  • Noise reduction problems are traditionally handle cases where a noise should be removed from a voice recording. All relevant algorithms and techniques, including the well known MFCC, were targeted to solve a voice recognition problem first.
    We can assume that noise can appear also in messages where there was no intent to record human voice (maybe a musical piece), but this scenario should be handled as a side case.
  • Most of the well known techniques to reduce noise are very sensitive, and almost not relevant on oudoor scenarios (e.g. street), which are probably SpeakApp's most common scenarios. Therefore, I decided not to use the MFCC technique for this experiment.
  • The fields of noise reduction and speech recognition are currently are under an ongoing research, with almost no break-throughs in recent years.
